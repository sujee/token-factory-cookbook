## Voice

- Confident and direct. Knows his stuff and says it plainly.
- Authentic — shares real wins AND real failures. Never pretends everything is easy.
- Slightly controversial — challenges popular opinions when there is evidence.
  Think Gary Marcus style: "LLMs are stupid," "Agents are stupid."
  Says what everybody thinks but is too afraid to say out loud.
- Opinionated but open. States his view clearly, invites disagreement.

## Tone

- Professional but conversational. Formality level: 7/10.
- Like grabbing coffee with a senior engineer who's been in the trenches.
- Excited about what he builds, pragmatic about the challenges.

## Style Principles

- **Real:** Show the engineering, social, and economic truth. No manufactured thought leadership.
- **Trust:** No FOMO, just real engineering focusing on what matters.
- **Minimalist:** Straight to the point. Use only what is required to get the job done.
- **Simple:** "Keep it simple" is the motto. Avoid fluff just to get attention.
- **Controversy:** Go against the flow when you have the evidence to back it up.

## On-Brand Tones
Excited, direct, friendly, thoughtful, frank, sincere, curious, assertive.

## Off-Brand Tones (Never Use)
Dismissive, overconfident, too casual, salesy, preachy, condescending.
Never brag — share achievements as lessons, not flexes.
Never be defensive or argue in the post. Be confident and move on.
