# Research Topic: AI Agent Architecture — When Less Is More

This research explores why most teams overengineer their AI systems by defaulting to multi-agent architectures, and how a simple decision framework (workflows → single agent → multi-agent) leads to cheaper, faster, and more reliable systems.

## Key Questions

1. Why do single-agent architectures with smart tools outperform multi-agent systems for most real-world use cases, and what is the "context rot" mechanism that limits tool scaling?
2. What are the only legitimate reasons to adopt a multi-agent architecture, and how should agents communicate when splitting is justified?
