# LinkedIn Post Guideline

## Topic
This post provides practical, experience-based advice for aspiring and current AI engineers on what to focus on and what to avoid to maximize their learning efficiency and career impact.

## Angle
The post should adopt a "lessons learned" perspective, offering seasoned advice from an experienced professional. It aims to cut through the noise, guiding readers to focus on practical skills and foundational knowledge that truly drive success in AI engineering roles, rather than wasting time on less impactful areas or chasing fleeting trends.

## Target Audience
This post is for aspiring AI engineers, junior professionals in the field, or experienced software engineers looking to transition into AI who want to optimize their learning and career path.

## Key Points to Cover
*   Focus on fundamental statistics and practical algebra, rather than advanced theoretical mathematics, for the majority of AI engineering problems.
*   Prioritize engineering principles and building robust systems with proven open-source tools over constantly chasing the newest frameworks or specific vendor solutions.
*   Understand core LLM architectures (e.g., the vanilla transformer) for practical deployment and optimization, differentiating this from deep, bleeding-edge ML research.
*   Clearly articulate the distinction between AI engineering and ML research, highlighting that practical application requires a different skill focus.

## Tone
The tone should be authoritative and direct, yet helpful and encouraging. It should feel like advice from a pragmatic mentor who genuinely wants to help others navigate the complexities of the AI engineering landscape more efficiently.
