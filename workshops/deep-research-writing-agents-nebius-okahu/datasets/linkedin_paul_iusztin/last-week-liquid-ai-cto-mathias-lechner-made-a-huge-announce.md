Last week, Liquid AI CTO Mathias Lechner made a huge announcement.

Their playbook for building small, efficient, on-device models is now open-source.

It's one of the boldest moves we've seen in the edge AI space this year...

Why?

Because for years, edge AI has been held back by a single constraint:

Most of what we know about training large models is well documented.
Most of what we need to know about training tiny, real-time models… isn’t.

This gap means teams are forced to reinvent the wheel every time they try to deploy intelligence on edge devices.

But Liquid AI's report sheds light on that missing piece.

It walks through:

• Hardware-in-the-loop architecture search techniques
• A distillation objective tailored to small-model training
• A 3-stage post-training pipeline optimized for edge efficiency
• LFM2-VL for vision-language tasks
• LFM2-Audio for real-time speech
• LFM2-ColBERT for multilingual retrieval

Edge AI isn’t going to be driven by one huge model sitting in the cloud...

It’s going to be driven by many small, specialized models running close to the user, with a larger model coordinating at the center.

For that to work, we need more transparency around how these small models are built, optimized, and distilled.

Releasing this level of detail moves the field forward.
