Many aspiring AI engineers waste months learning the wrong things.
They get caught up in theory that won't help them ship a single product.

Forget chasing advanced theoretical math. You need practical statistics and basic linear algebra.
Focus on understanding distributions, hypothesis testing, and matrix operations.
That covers 90% of what you'll actually use day-to-day.

Stop chasing the newest AI framework every month.
Focus on core software engineering principles.
Think system design, clean code, and building **solid systems**.

Master proven open-source tools: Docker, Metaflow, Airflow, Terraform.
Learn cloud platforms like AWS or GCP.

You don't need to be an ML researcher to build with LLMs.

Understand the **vanilla Transformer architecture** for deployment and optimization.
Know *how* it works, not just *why* every new research paper gets buzz.
That means understanding attention mechanisms and positional encodings.

AI engineering is about shipping working AI products.
ML research is about creating new algorithms and models.

These are different jobs with different skill sets.
Your time is better spent building, iterating, and deploying.

Prioritize shipping over endless studying. That’s how you learn.
