# Research Topic: AI Agent Architecture Decision Frameworks
This research explores best practices and common pitfalls in designing AI agent architectures, focusing on how to choose appropriate system complexity and autonomy levels for real-world applications. The goal is to understand strategies for avoiding inefficient or over-engineered solutions.

## Key Questions

1.  What are the key differentiating factors and ideal use cases for workflows, single AI agents, and multi-agent systems in modern AI development?
2.  How do architectural choices, particularly regarding unnecessary autonomy, impact system cost, latency, and debugging effort in AI applications?
3.  What decision frameworks or methodologies can guide developers in selecting the optimal AI agent architecture based on specific project requirements and constraints?
4.  What are established principles for designing robust AI agent systems, including the integration of thin agents with heavy tools, validation loops, observability, and human-in-the-loop (HITL) mechanisms?
5.  How can an "Autonomy Test" or similar evaluation help determine the genuine need for an AI agent versus simpler deterministic solutions, and when do multi-agent systems become truly justified?
