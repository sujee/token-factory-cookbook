In 2023, it was predicted that AI would replace up to 80% of developers by 2025.

Here's what happened in reality:

Tech companies are now (quietly) hiring more developers than ever before.

(Including the ones that laid off most of their workforce in anticipation)

Is AI failing? Of course not.

Companies have just realized it’s capabilities were overestimated.

Yes, AI can write code.

But even the most amazing tools out there do not compare to the problem-solving skills of a human.

So when code is offloaded from a human, it creates a hidden tax in review, security, and maintenance.

I’ve said it before and I’ll say it again..

AI isn’t replacing programming.
But it is rewriting what it means to be a programmer.

We no longer have the knowledge bottleneck.
AI handles that quite well.

This means the barrier to entry for development has dropped.

For example, some months back explored building a SQL dialect for GraphRAG.

I’m not a SQL expert, but with AI as my assistant, I could experiment, iterate, and make progress that would’ve taken weeks otherwise.

The catch? I know the fundamentals of development.

This is the only reason I was able to learn while I build.

In other words, AI has created a new paradigm for developers…

“Build, and learn as you go.”

But it will NOT replace us anytime soon.

It’s most effective when added to strong development fundamentals.

And this is our competitive advantage as developers.
