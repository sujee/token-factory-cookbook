# Research Topic: Building a Personal AI Second Brain with Structured Memory

This research aims to understand the architectural components and technical considerations involved in designing a personalized AI agent capable of reasoning over an individual's accumulated knowledge, memories, and preferences to assist with cognitive tasks. The focus is on leveraging structured memory and reliable orchestration.

## Key Questions

1.  What are the core architectural patterns and principles for building a "Second Brain" AI agent that processes and utilizes personal data?
2.  How does GraphRAG (Graph-based Retrieval Augmented Generation) function as a foundation for knowledge ingestion and retrieval, including entity extraction, relationship mapping, and multiple retrieval modes?
3.  What are the key capabilities and design considerations for an agentic layer that interacts with a knowledge graph, updates different types of long-term memory, and integrates external tools and specialized LLMs?
4.  How do workflow orchestration tools ensure durability, scalability, and reliability across the different stages of an AI agent's pipeline, from data ingestion to agent inference?
5.  What is the concept of an MCP server, and how does it enable interaction and serving of AI agents to various client applications for conversational refinement?
