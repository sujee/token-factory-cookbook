People see the AI engineer, the author, the founder. They don't see the kid who almost wasted it all.

For years, my own life was a mess of self-sabotage. I had talent, but I spent it on bad habits and worse company.

I was stuck. Broke. Running in circles. My public persona hid a different reality.

The turning point wasn't an epiphany. It was a hard look at my path. I saw the patterns.

**Decisions had to be made.**

I cut ties with old friends.

Stopped chasing quick wins.

Started building real skills.

Said no to distractions.

I chose boredom over chaos.

It was painful and lonely. But it was the only way to build something real. From that mess, I learned lessons that changed everything:

*   **Change your environment.** Ruthlessly cut out toxic surroundings and inputs.
*   **Discipline is just showing up.** Small, consistent actions compound.
*   Stop comparing your messy start to someone else's polished end. Focus on your own progress.
*   Treat your life like a system. Never stop learning, never stop iterating.

My journey wasn't magic. It was deliberate choices, hard work, and a constant drive to improve.
