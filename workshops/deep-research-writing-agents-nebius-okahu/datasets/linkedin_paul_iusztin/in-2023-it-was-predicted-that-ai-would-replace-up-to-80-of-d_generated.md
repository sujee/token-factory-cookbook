It was widely predicted by industry analysts that AI would replace up to 80% of developer jobs by 2025.

This prediction has been proven to be substantially incorrect; the reality is far more nuanced than what was initially anticipated by the experts.

The developer job market has been transformed by AI, but not in the way that was expected. Code is being generated by AI tools; however, new challenges are being introduced simultaneously.

Hidden taxes are being imposed on development teams:
- Code quality must be reviewed more carefully by senior engineers
- Security vulnerabilities are being introduced by AI-generated code at an alarming rate; this creates significant risk
- Long-term maintenance burden is being increased substantially

One's role as a developer is being redefined. The barrier to entry is being lowered by AI; meanwhile, fundamental skills are being made more valuable than ever.

It should be noted that the competitive advantage lies not in the volume of code that can be produced; rather, it is found in the depth of understanding that one brings to complex systems.
