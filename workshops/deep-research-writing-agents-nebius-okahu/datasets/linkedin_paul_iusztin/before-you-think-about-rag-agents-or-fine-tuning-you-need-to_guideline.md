# LinkedIn Post Guideline

## Topic
This post is about the critical importance of a well-designed data pipeline architecture as the foundation for building effective and scalable AI systems, especially AI assistants.

## Angle
The post should challenge the common focus on advanced AI algorithms (RAG, agents, fine-tuning) by asserting that data quality and pipeline architecture are more fundamental. It should offer a practical, layered approach to building such a pipeline, illustrated with a real-world example, emphasizing principles like data standardization, quality scoring, and decoupling data from AI layers.

## Target Audience
This post is for AI/ML engineers, data scientists, and developers who are building or architecting AI-powered systems and assistants, particularly those interested in practical infrastructure design.

## Key Points to Cover
*   Emphasize that a strong data pipeline architecture is paramount for AI success, preceding considerations of advanced AI techniques.
*   Outline a two-layered data pipeline approach: raw data collection/storage and a subsequent ETL process.
*   Highlight the importance of data standardization (e.g., to Markdown) and quality scoring for ensuring data usability and performance of AI systems.
*   Explain the principle of decoupling data and AI layers for flexibility and iterative development.
*   Reference a personal project or an AI assistant as a concrete example where these principles were applied.

## Tone
The tone should be authoritative, insightful, and practical, offering clear guidance. It should also be slightly provocative by questioning common priorities in AI development, while remaining educational and encouraging.
