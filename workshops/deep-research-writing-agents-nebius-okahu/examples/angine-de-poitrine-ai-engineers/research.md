Angine de Poitrine is a Canadian experimental rock duo from Saguenay, Quebec, known for their unique musical style, custom microtonal instrumentation, and distinctive anonymity costumes. The band, formed in 2019, consists of two members who perform under the pseudonyms Khn de Poitrine and Klek de Poitrine. The name "Angine de Poitrine" translates to "angina pectoris," a medical term for chest pain, which the duo states reflects the tension and release present in their music. They describe their artistic output as a "Mantra-Rock Dada Pythago-Cubist Orchestra."

**Khn Klek and Anonymity Costumes**
The duo is comprised of Khn de Poitrine, who handles guitar, bass, and looping, and Klek de Poitrine, the drummer. They have chosen to remain anonymous, concealing their real identities and performing solely under their pseudonyms. Their commitment to anonymity extends to their public appearances, where they reportedly communicate only in a made-up, alien-like language.

Their anonymity is reinforced by elaborate and visually striking costumes. These include oversized papier-mâché masks featuring long, distinctive noses, along with white and black polka-dot body paint applied to their exposed skin, such as their hands and bare feet. Klek's drum kit is also covered with fabric, serving both aesthetic and acoustic purposes by dampening the drum's resonance for a tighter sound. The use of these costumes and their anonymity began as an inside joke but was maintained to allow the musicians to pursue other individual projects and perform within their local scene without recognition. Klek views this practice as a philosophical and social experiment, challenging conventional ideas of success and validation.

**Custom Double-Necked Microtonal Guitar Instrument**
A central element of Angine de Poitrine's sound is Khn de Poitrine's custom double-necked microtonal guitar. This unique instrument is a hybrid, combining a Fender Stratocaster-like guitar with a bass, both wired separately. What truly distinguishes it are the additional microtonal frets, which allow Khn to play tones that fall between the standard 12 notes of Western music. The instrument is designed to divide each octave into 24 notes, effectively utilizing quarter tones.

Initially, Klek experimented by modifying a regular guitar with extra frets using a saw to create a microtonal instrument, which then led to the development of Khn's custom guitar. The custom instrument was built by a luthier in Saguenay and reportedly took over 150 hours to complete. It features oversized, phosphorescent fret markers on the side and top of the necks, a necessary adaptation to compensate for Khn's vision impairment while wearing his mask. Specifically, the guitar neck incorporates 30 quarter-tone frets and 6 semitone frets, while the bass neck has 24 quarter-tone frets and 8 semitone frets. Khn utilizes a looping pedal during live performances to layer intricate guitar and bass lines in real-time, creating complex and dense soundscapes that are influenced by genres such as progressive rock, modern jazz, acid techno, disco, and various forms of East, South, and West Asian music.
