LinkedIn is drowning in AI advice.
And honestly, I find most of it is noise.

You see countless posts about "AI strategies" or "prompt mastery" from people who barely built anything in production. I see it as an endless stream of surface-level takes.

This isn't surprising. There’s a psychological effect at play.

When you’re new to a complex field like AI, you often overestimate your knowledge. It feels simple at first. That's the **"Peak of Mount Stupid"**.

So, you talk a lot. You share big, confident claims.

But true experts? The ones who actually build, deploy, and debug AI systems?
They know how much they don’t know. They understand the real complexities, the limitations, the ethical minefields.

They're usually quieter, more **thoughtful**. They live in the **"Valley of Despair"** for a good reason.

These are the builders, the researchers, the engineers in the trenches. They share insights based on tangible experience, not just for algorithm points. They talk about real problems, real solutions, and often, real failures.

They don’t promise magic. They show the engineering truth.

I find it's getting harder to find these genuine voices. The platforms amplify confidence, not necessarily competence.

Cut through the noise. Seek out people who are actively building, testing, and getting their hands dirty.

Look for the messy details and the lessons from shipping. That’s where true learning happens.
