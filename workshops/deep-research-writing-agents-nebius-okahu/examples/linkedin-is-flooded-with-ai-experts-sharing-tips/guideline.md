# LinkedIn Post Guideline

## Topic
This post is about cutting through the noise of AI advice on LinkedIn to identify and follow truly impactful experts and builders.

## Angle
The post should take a critical yet helpful stance, highlighting the prevalence of superficial AI advice on LinkedIn due to less experienced individuals oversharing. It should emphasize the value of following genuine builders and researchers who share insights backed by real-world experience, and explain the psychological phenomenon (e.g., Dunning-Kruger effect) that contributes to this dynamic.

## Target Audience
This post is for professionals, researchers, and enthusiasts in AI, data science, and related tech fields who are seeking high-quality, actionable insights and struggling to identify credible voices amidst the vast amount of content on LinkedIn.

## Key Points to Cover
*   Acknowledge the current overload of AI-related tips and advice on LinkedIn, much of which may lack depth.
*   Explain the phenomenon behind why less experienced individuals might overconfidently share, while true experts tend to be more reserved (e.g., Dunning-Kruger effect).
*   Describe the characteristics of genuine AI experts: they are actively building, testing, and sharing insights based on proven experience, not for algorithmic gain.
*   Encourage the audience to seek out and follow these authentic voices for true learning and growth.
*   Call to action for the audience to share recommendations of other genuine builders and thinkers in AI.

## Tone
The tone should be discerning and insightful, with a helpful and slightly critical edge towards superficial content, but ultimately constructive and community-oriented.
